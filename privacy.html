Privacy Policy

This policy explains how Headshift: 2048 Snake Puzzle handles your data.

Last updated: 2026-07-23

Product model

Headshift: 2048 Snake Puzzle ("Headshift") is a free, single-player, offline puzzle game published by Goethe47 (package com.goethe47.headshift). It has no in-app purchases and no subscriptions. It shows ads, described below.

Account model

Headshift does not require an account, login, or account creation. There is no server-side player profile of any kind. Because there is no Headshift account, there is no account deletion URL.

Data stored only on your device

The following is stored locally in the app's own storage on your device and is never sent to us — we do not operate a game server:

  • Best score, so your local high score persists between sessions.
  • Language selection, so the app opens in your chosen language next time.
  • Sound on/off preference.
  • Whether you've completed the first-launch tutorial.
  • Your ad-consent choice (see "Your choices" below).
  • Ad-frequency bookkeeping (a count of completed games and the timestamp of the last interstitial ad), used only to avoid showing ads too often.

Uninstalling the app, or clearing its storage in Android Settings, removes all of this.

Advertising

Headshift is ad-supported. Ads are delivered through Unity LevelPlay (formerly ironSource), a mediation platform that can route ad requests to a number of certified advertising network partners. Which partner networks are active can change over time without an app update, since this is configured on Unity's mediation dashboard rather than in the app itself.

To serve, measure, and (where you've consented) personalize ads, Unity LevelPlay and its partner networks may collect or access, via the Android system:

  • Your advertising ID (a resettable, user-controllable device identifier for ads).
  • General device information (OS version, device model, and similar).
  • Your IP address, used for approximate (city/region-level) location and fraud prevention, not precise GPS location — Headshift never requests location permission.
  • App interaction data related to ad delivery (e.g. which ads were shown or clicked), and, since Android 13+, data via Google's Privacy Sandbox Topics and Attribution APIs for interest-based ad delivery and conversion measurement without cross-app device tracking.

This data is collected and processed by Unity and its advertising partners under their own privacy policies, not by Goethe47 directly. See Unity's Player and App User Privacy Policy for details on the LevelPlay/ironSource platform.

Your choices

The first time you open Headshift, you're shown a screen asking whether you'd like personalized or non-personalized ads before any ad SDK is initialized. Your choice is stored locally on your device (see above) and used to configure the consent signal Headshift passes to Unity LevelPlay.

This is a simple, self-built consent screen — it is not a certified IAB Transparency & Consent Framework (TCF) CMP. At present there is no in-app control to change your choice after the first launch; to reset it, clear the app's storage or reinstall the app, which will show the consent screen again.

You may also have rights under GDPR, UK GDPR, or the CCPA/CPRA (e.g. to access, delete, or opt out of the sale/sharing of data) with respect to data Unity and its ad partners process about you. These requests are best directed to those companies directly, using the contact details in their own privacy policies, since Goethe47 does not hold or control that data.

Permissions used

Headshift itself requests no permissions. The permissions present in the installed app come from the advertising SDK and include: Internet access and network state (to load ads), advertising ID access, and Android's Privacy Sandbox (Topics/Attribution) permissions described above. Headshift does not request camera, microphone, contacts, storage, or location permissions.

Children's privacy

Headshift is not directed at children under 13 and Goethe47 does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the app's advertising, please contact us so we can address it.

What Headshift does not do

  • No accounts, login, or user profiles.
  • No collection of your name, email, or contact details.
  • No access to your messages, contacts, photos, files, or precise location.
  • No sale of data by Goethe47 — we don't hold any data to sell in the first place.

Security

Locally stored data is protected by standard Android app sandboxing. No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure, and this applies to our advertising partners' systems as well as anyone else's.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app or its advertising partners change. Material changes will be reflected here with an updated "Last updated" date.
