Privacy Policy

This policy explains how Headshift handles your data. It describes the app's actual current behavior, not a future plan.

Developer / data controller

Goethe47 is the developer and, for the data described in this policy, the controller. Contact: burteloffluther@gmail.com. Package: com.goethe47.headshift.

Product model

Headshift: 2048 Snake Puzzle ("Headshift") is a free, single-player puzzle game. It has no in-app purchases and no subscriptions. It is ad-supported, described below.

Account model

Headshift does not require an account, login, or account creation. There is no server-side player profile of any kind. Because there is no Headshift account, there is no account deletion URL — see "Deletion and local storage" below for what deletion actually means for this app.

Core gameplay and internet use

Core gameplay and local progress work without a game server. Headshift does not require an internet connection to be played. Internet access is used to load advertising and related privacy services (see below).

Data stored locally on your device

The following is stored only in the app's own local storage on your device. Goethe47 does not operate a game server, so none of this is transmitted to us:

  • Best score, so your local high score persists between sessions.
  • Language selection.
  • Sound on/off preference.
  • Whether you've completed the first-launch tutorial.
  • Your advertising privacy choice (personalized ads or non-personalized ads only — ads are shown either way) and your US-state do-not-sell-or-share choice — see "Advertising and privacy choices" below.
  • Ad-frequency bookkeeping (a count of completed games and the timestamp of the last interstitial ad), used only to avoid showing ads too often.

This local data by itself is not a "collection" in the Google Play Data Safety sense — it never leaves your device unless you enable personalized/non-personalized ads, at which point the advertising data described below is what actually gets transmitted, by the advertising SDK, not by this stored data itself.

Advertising services actually included

Headshift shows ads through exactly two components, both confirmed present in the app's build: Unity LevelPlay (Unity's mediation SDK) running the Unity Ads network via its Unity Ads adapter. No other ad network's SDK or adapter is included in the app. Unity's dashboard lets specific ad placements/instances change without an app update, but that is not the same as adding a new network — a genuinely new advertising network requires adding its adapter to the build, which would mean a new app version and an update to this policy and to Play Console's Data safety section before it goes out.

Categories of advertising data

Based on Unity's own official Data Safety documentation for Unity Ads and on the permissions actually present in Headshift's built app, using the ad SDKs above may involve:

  • Approximate location (from IP address) — for ad delivery, fraud prevention, and analytics; not precise GPS location, which Headshift never requests.
  • Device or other identifiers — the Android advertising ID and similar SDK-level device identifiers.
  • App interactions within the ad itself — Unity's own documentation specifies this is limited to taps/views inside the rendered ad, not Headshift's gameplay screens, which no ad or analytics SDK observes.
  • Ad-serving diagnostics (e.g. SDK version, network connectivity for ad requests) — distinct from game crash logs or performance data, which no SDK in this app collects.
  • SDK-internal identifiers Unity Ads' own systems may use for fraud prevention and ad accounting — not a Headshift account or login, which don't exist.

Headshift's build also declares Android's Privacy Sandbox Topics and Attribution permissions (confirmed by inspecting the built app's manifest). A declared permission means the advertising library is capable of using that API; Goethe47 has not independently inspected the closed-source SDK to confirm exactly when it does. This declaration is treated as a "yes" for Data Safety purposes, per Google's own guidance that advertising SDK data counts as collected.

Advertising and privacy choices

Headshift is an ad-supported app — ads are shown regardless of which privacy choice you make; the choice controls personalization, not whether ads run. The first time you open Headshift, before any advertising SDK is started, you're asked to choose one of two options: personalized ads, which use ad data to select what's shown, or non-personalized ads only, which are still shown but not selected using your data. Both options start Unity LevelPlay and Unity Ads; the only difference is the consent signal passed to them.

You can review or change this choice at any time from Settings → Privacy Choices — no data clearing or reinstall needed. Because the advertising SDK cannot be fully re-initialized once started, changing your choice (or the CCPA toggle below) instead destroys and recreates the banner and interstitial ad objects under the new signal, so an ad already fetched under your previous choice cannot still be shown after you change it — without creating duplicate ad instances or calling the SDK's initializer twice.

US-state privacy choices

Settings → Privacy Choices also includes a "Do not sell or share my info" toggle, implementing the do-not-sell/share signal (CCPA/CPRA and similar US state laws) that Headshift passes to Unity LevelPlay. It's offered to every player rather than limited to a specific detected state, since this app has no region-detection mechanism — offering the control more broadly than strictly required isn't a compliance problem. Whether CCPA/CPRA or another state law specifically applies to your relationship with Goethe47 depends on factors this policy can't fully evaluate for every reader; the toggle is available regardless.

Retention

Data processed by Unity/Unity Ads is retained per Unity's own policy, not a schedule Goethe47 sets. Data stored locally on your device (see above) is retained until you clear the app's storage, uninstall the app, or change the relevant setting yourself.

Deletion and local storage

Headshift's Android Auto Backup is disabled for this app (android:allowBackup="false", confirmed in the built app's manifest). This one setting is what stops both cloud backup and device-to-device transfer of the app's local data — Android skips both mechanisms outright when it's false. Uninstalling the app — or clearing its storage in Android Settings — actually removes all of the locally stored data listed above, including your advertising privacy choice; none of it is restored on reinstall, and none of it was ever eligible for backup in the first place. Because Headshift has no account and no server, there is no separate "delete my account" step — uninstalling is the local deletion mechanism. For data already processed by Unity/Unity Ads, use the contact/rights information above.

Android permissions

Headshift's own game code does not use camera, microphone, contacts, files, or device-location permissions. The app's advertising libraries add internet access, network-state, advertising-identifier, and Android Privacy Sandbox (Topics/Attribution) permissions to the final installed app, plus a small number of supporting Android system-service permissions (e.g. for scheduling background work) that these libraries need to function — these support the advertising SDK's own operation and are not, by themselves, evidence of an additional personal-data category beyond what's listed above.

The app's advertising configuration reflects this as a fixed setting, not a per-player choice.
